True Crime

Welcome to The Insomniac’s true crime corner! Below are this week’s recommendations of documentaries, podcasts, and books to satisfy all of your true crime needs.

>

--------------------------

Podcast: My Favorite Murder with Karen Kilgariff and Georgia Hardstark
This incredible podcast combines true crime and comedy as the two hosts recount true crime cases to one another. They also discuss home town murders sent in by listeners. Check it out if you enjoy your tales of murder with a little side of laughter. If you fancy something a bit more serious, listen to True Crime Garage!



Documentary: The Central Park Five, 2012
This documentary, directed by Ken Burns, examines the case of five men who were wrongfully convicted of raping a woman in Central Park. After serving around ten years in prison each, a serial rapist confessed to the crime, resulting in overturned convictions for the five men. The documentary examines what role racial tensions and the media played in this great miscarriage of justice.



Mini-Series: The Staircase, 2005
Was it cold blooded murder, or do you believe the theory that an owl is to blame? This eight part series follows the case and trail of Michael Peterson and the mysterious death of his wife, whom he is accused of having murdered. Check it out, if only to hear the crazy theories that surround the case.



Book: My Friend Dahmer by Derf Backdorf
This is a deeply unsettling- yet alarmingly captivating- graphic novel written by a high school acquaintance of the notorious serial killer Jeffery Dahmer. The graphic novel is an examination of the nature and condition of Dahmer’s formative teenage years and the various circumstances which shaped him into the monster he became.



Film: Zodiac, 2007
Based on the true case from the 1970s, this film, starring Jake Gyllenhaal, Robert Downey Jr., and Mark Ruffalo, follows investigators and reporters from the San Francisco newspaper as they try to track down and capture the Zodiac killer, a serial murderer who terrorized Northern California and evaded capture. The Zodiac Killer sent letters to the local newspaper detailing the crimes, as well as cryptic codes that have yet to be deciphered.



Hungry for more? Check out the following:
Documentary: West of Memphis (2012)
TV Series: Making A Murderer (2015)
Books: Helter Skelter by Vincent Bugliosi, In Cold Blood by Truman Capote,
and Devil in the White City by Erik Larson
